## 📑 Methodology spotlight: Preferential attachment & the winner-take-most regime

When the rate at which a node gains connections is proportional to its current degree, the system produces a heavy-tailed degree distribution — the *rich-get-richer* phenomenon. In markets, this maps to network effects: platforms with the most users attract the most developers, attracting more users.

The structural signature is power-law share distribution + low switching elasticity. The risk signature is *winner concentration*: the system is brittle to events that disrupt the dominant node, because no substitute has been allowed to scale.
