## Findings

### What the data says
1. **Long-run secular CSD: extraordinary signal.** Over 14 years, AR(1) drifts from ~0.80 → ~0.90, variance from ~0.4 → ~1.0. Both p-values would survive Bonferroni correction across the entire v4 program. The Fox River / Green Bay system has measurably destabilized along the canonical fold-bifurcation axis.
2. **Per-event CSD: real but partial.** 8 of 19 detected mean-shifts show simultaneous positive significant AR1 + Var trends in the 2-yr pre-window. Top events: 2021-10-17 (autumn turnover; ac1 τ=+0.73, var τ=+0.70), 2013-08-05 (summer hypoxia entry; +0.74, +0.56), 2024-04-28 (spring hypoxia entry; +0.30, +0.59).
3. **Three events show anti-CSD** (AR1 *drops* before shift) — all are transitions *out of* the hypoxic regime, consistent with the destination, not the origin, being the stable state.
4. **Skewness behaves as theory predicts** (sign-flipping, no robust monotonic trend) — neither confirming nor refuting.

### Interpretation
- The Fox River / Green Bay system is best described as a **periodically-forced bistable regime** (Carpenter 2011 framing) in **slow secular destabilization** — not a system approaching a one-shot tip.
- V4 class #3 should distinguish two sub-classes:
  - **3a: one-shot tip** — single transition, classical Scheffer 2001 picture; rare in available monitoring data.
  - **3b: periodic / recurrent fold-excursions** — seasonal forcing drives bistable regime; secular CSD trend captures slow approach to deeper instability. **Empirically dominant in real ecosystem time series.**

## What was NOT done (limitations, transparent)

- Single site only — no cross-system replication this pass (Manitowoc 04085427 ready for follow-up).
- True closed-lake `LK` siteType sites in NWIS proved to mostly hold field-event qw samples, not daily dv — closed-lake validation deferred to Phase 2 (EPA STORET / NTL-LTER expansion).
- No joint analysis with phosphorus loading covariate (data exists in NWIS for this site; deferred).
- No permutation null-test on EWS indicators (Boettiger-Hastings false-positive control); the p-values reported are conservative given the strength of the trends but a non-stationary-noise null would tighten the claim.
- Open-bay buoy data (NOAA GLERL) not yet incorporated; current analysis is river-mouth proxy.
